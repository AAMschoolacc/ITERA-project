BASALTIC

Basaltic rocks on the Moon are similar in composition to basaltic rocks found on Earth. They are dark in color and are rich in iron and magnesium.

Lunar basalts are primarily composed of minerals such as pyroxenes, plagioclase feldspar, and olivine.

These rocks are primarily the result of ancient volcanic activity on the Moon’s surface and are commonly found in the lunar maria.

ANORTOSITHIC

Anorthositic rocks are light in color and are predominantly composed of a mineral called plagioclase feldspar.

They are mainly composed of plagioclase feldspar, with smaller amounts of other minerals.

BRECCIAS

Lunar breccias are composite rocks formed from fragments of various rock types and impact-generated materials that have been fused together.

Breccias can contain a mixture of basaltic and anorthositic components, along with other minerals and impact melt.

They are formed as a result of meteorite impacts on the Moon and are valuable for studying lunar impact history.

REGOLITH

Lunar regolith is not a single rock type but a layer of loose, fragmented material covering the Moon’s surface.

It consists of a mixture of small rock and mineral fragments, dust, and tiny glass beads formed by meteorite impacts.

Regolith is the surface material of the Moon and provides insights into lunar surface conditions and the history of impacts.

IMPACT MELT ROCKS

These rocks are formed when the intense heat generated by meteorite impacts causes surrounding rocks to melt and then solidify.

Impact melt rocks can contain a mix of various lunar minerals and glassy material.

They are a product of high-velocity impacts on the Moon and are valuable for understanding impact processes.

KREEP

KREEP rocks are enriched in potassium (K), rare earth elements (REE), and phosphorus (P). They are relatively rare on the lunar surface.

They contain a variety of minerals but are distinguished by their enrichment in K, REE, and P.

KREEP materials are thought to represent concentrated deposits in certain areas of the Moon’s crust.
