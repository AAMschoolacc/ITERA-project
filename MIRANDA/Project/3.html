THIRD FACT ABOUT THE MOON

DID YOU KNOW?

The moon always shows the same side to the Earth, because it rotates on its own axis at the same rate as it orbits the Earth. This is called synchronous rotation or tidal locking. The side that faces the Earth is called the near side, while the side that faces away is called the far side. The far side was first seen by humans in 1959, when the Soviet spacecraft Luna 3 took pictures of it
